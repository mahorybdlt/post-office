What is this place?

A landmark still alive today

Built in the late nineteenth century, the Central Post Office reflects French colonial influence while remaining an active public building and one of the city’s best-known attractions.

What you are seeing

Key details around you

🏛️

The main hall

A large open interior inspired by European railway stations, designed to feel bright and monumental.

🧱

Arches and structure

The metal framework and repeated arches reflect the elegance of late colonial architecture.

🖼️

Portrait of Ho Chi Minh

Inside the building, this portrait connects the monument to modern Vietnamese history and identity.

📐

Symmetrical layout

Its balanced composition helps create a strong sense of order, openness and visual harmony.

History in brief

Three moments to remember

1890s
The building was constructed during the French colonial period.
20th century
It became one of the best-known civic landmarks in the center of Saigon.
Today
It remains both a working post office and a major destination for visitors.
